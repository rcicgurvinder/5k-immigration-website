COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an RCIC and why does it matter? ×

An RCIC (Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant) is a licensed professional authorized by the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants to represent clients in Canadian immigration matters. Working with an RCIC means you have a licensed professional handling your file — not an unlicensed ghost consultant operating without oversight.

How long does the immigration process take? ×

Processing times vary widely depending on the program, your country of origin, and IRCC processing volumes. During your consultation, I'll provide realistic timelines based on current processing standards for your specific situation.

Do you guarantee my application will be approved? ×

No honest consultant can guarantee approval. The final decision is always made by IRCC. What I can promise is that your application will be carefully prepared, fully documented, and put together in the best way possible to give you a strong chance of success.

What happens during the initial consultation? ×

During our consultation, I'll review your background, assess your eligibility for different programs, explain your options clearly, and outline a recommended strategy. You'll leave with a clear understanding of your next steps and realistic expectations.

How are your fees structured? ×

Fees are transparent and outlined in a detailed service agreement before any work begins. There are no hidden charges. You'll know exactly what you're paying for and what services are included at each stage of your case.
