Privacy Policy

How 5K Immigration Services collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Effective Date: April 28, 2026

5K Immigration Services ("we," "our," or "us") is a regulated immigration consulting practice based in Surrey, British Columbia, Canada. We are committed to protecting the privacy of our clients and website visitors. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard personal information in compliance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and British Columbia's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA), as well as our obligations under the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants (CICC) Code of Professional Conduct.

1. Information We Collect

We collect personal information only to the extent necessary to provide our immigration consulting services. This may include:

  • Full legal name, date of birth, and nationality
  • Contact details — mailing address, phone number, email address
  • Passport and travel document details
  • Immigration history, visa refusals, and prior status in Canada
  • Employment history, job titles, and reference letters
  • Educational credentials and ECA reports
  • Language test results (IELTS, CELPIP, TEF, TCF, etc.)
  • Family information relevant to sponsorship or dependent applications
  • Financial records provided in support of an application
  • Police certificates and court records (where applicable)
  • Medical examination results provided by an IRCC panel physician
  • Communications, notes, and correspondence related to your file

When you use our website, we may also collect standard technical data such as IP address, browser type, pages visited, and session duration through basic analytics tools.

2. How We Collect Your Information

We collect personal information directly from you through:

  • Initial consultation bookings and intake forms
  • Email, telephone, and in-person communications
  • Documents you provide or authorize us to obtain on your behalf
  • Online forms on our website (contact, booking)

We do not collect personal information without your knowledge or consent, except where permitted or required by law.

3. Purpose of Collection and Use

We use your personal information solely for the purposes for which it was collected, including:

  • Providing immigration consulting services and preparing applications
  • Submitting applications to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC), Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC), Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA), and other relevant government bodies
  • Communicating with you about your file, deadlines, and outcomes
  • Fulfilling our regulatory obligations as a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC) under the CICC Code of Professional Conduct
  • Maintaining accurate client records as required by our governing body
  • Improving our services and responding to inquiries

We will not use your information for any purpose other than those listed above without your explicit consent.

4. Disclosure of Personal Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may disclose your information only in the following circumstances:

  • Government authorities: IRCC, ESDC, CBSA, and provincial regulatory bodies as required by the nature of your application
  • Authorized third parties: Professionals engaged on your behalf (e.g., translators, medical clinics, panel physicians) under strict confidentiality obligations
  • Legal requirements: When required by a court order, subpoena, or applicable law
  • CICC audits: Our files may be subject to review by the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants as part of our regulatory obligations

We will never share your personal information with unauthorized third parties, marketers, or immigration agents outside of our practice without your written consent.

5. Retention of Records

In accordance with CICC regulations, we retain client files for a minimum of six (6) years from the date the retainer agreement is concluded or the file is closed. After this period, personal information is securely destroyed or de-identified.

Website analytics data is retained only as long as necessary for the purposes described and is not linked to your identity.

6. Security Safeguards

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from loss, theft, unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification. Our safeguards include:

  • Secure, password-protected file management systems
  • Encrypted email communications for sensitive documents
  • Restricted access — only authorized staff handle client files
  • Physical security for any paper-based documents

No transmission over the internet is completely secure. While we take every reasonable precaution, we cannot guarantee the absolute security of information transmitted to us electronically.

7. Cookies and Website Analytics

Our website uses basic cookies and analytics tools to understand how visitors interact with our site. This data is aggregated and anonymous — it does not identify you personally. You may disable cookies through your browser settings; doing so will not affect your ability to use our services.

We do not use cookies to track you across other websites or to serve targeted advertising.

8. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction: Request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Withdrawal of consent: Withdraw consent to our use of your information at any time — note that this may limit our ability to provide or continue services
  • File a complaint: Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC) at priv.gc.ca or the BC Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner at oipc.bc.ca

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information below. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Links to External Websites

Our website may contain links to external sites, including the official IRCC website and other government resources. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their policies independently.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, regulatory requirements, or applicable law. The "Effective Date" at the top of this page indicates when the policy was last revised. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Continued use of our services after any update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
